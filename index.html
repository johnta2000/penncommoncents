Why personal finance literacy

Many Penn undergraduates are uninformed about important topics of “daily life” (i.e. credit score, savings/checking accounts, and stocks) whilst not realizing that one of the biggest contributors to credit score, for example, is time. It is extremely important that undergraduates, even in their years of infancy financials, understand even the most basic ways to manipulate their existing savings.

With the proper education, you could make about $500 off of just $5,000 in savings over the course of 5 years. However, many people do not know about these resources and miss out on simple opportunities to make the most of their dollar.

Who we are

We are Penn undergraduates from the College of Arts and Sciences, Engineering, and Wharton seeking to educate fellow classmates about the importance and basics of financial literacy. We are partnered with graduate club Wharton Common Cents with the common goal of advancing the current state of financial literacy here at Penn.

What we do

We host student-led lectures, guest speakers from major companies, and collaborative discussions to cover topics such as managing credit score, trading stocks (index/mutual funds, standard trades, etc.), opening checking & savings accounts, and more!
